Project Description

    This past summer, I was awarded an NSERC scholarship to work with Dr. John Tsotos, in his computational vision lab at York University. Dr. Tsotsos lab covers a variety of topics, including computational neuroscience, computational vision, robotics, and more. I was intrigued by his lab because of a past project he had worked on called Play Bot – an autonomous wheelchair meant for children with disabilities. The project had gone stagnant for a few years, and I was excited to start it up again.

    John and I had decided that the target for this new version of Play Bot should be elders. With a growing baby boomer generation, our goal was to mitigate some of the pressures on personal support workers in elderly care homes. Hence, the proposal for this project was to create an autonomous wheelchair, that could move between the rooms of a senior’s home; bedrooms, dining rooms, common areas.

    Senior homes were chosen due to their standardized design, and limited use of elevators. Navigation and localization would therefore be simpler for the wheelchair, making the primary focus obstacle avoidance. In my research, I discovered that no database currently exists of items encountered in a senior home setting, so I spent my time collecting and labelling images, and training a model on these images.

    Next steps for this project include completing the training for this model, integrating it into an existing ROS framework for the wheelchair, and testing it within the space. Another future goal would be to create a user interface that allows senior users to be completely independent when using SeniorBot, improving quality of life for seniors and personal support workers alike.

     I submitted my summer work to the Lassonde Undergraduate Summer Student Research Conference (LUSSRC) where I was chosen, as one of five students, to present my work through an oral presentation. I was awarded second place for this presentation.
