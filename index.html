About me...

Always a tinkerer and problem solver, I never saw computer science as an option for me. I would dip my toe in every couple of years, only to stray back to my comfortable path; science, psychology, and healthcare. I knew I wanted to help people, and though this path didn’t always feel right, it was a traditional way to get into a helping career.

After graduating, I was accepted into an entrepreneurial fellowship called Venture For Canada, which introduced me to tech and startups. I began working at a medical education start-up, at an innovation hub in Toronto. I was exhilarated by all the passionate people doing high impact work in so many different fields. However, as a sales person at the time, I didn’t feel like the impact I was making was in line with my personal desires.

After about two years I finally decided to dive in to software completely. I am now a student at York University studying computer science. I’ve taken courses in C, Java, JavaScript, User Experience, Computer Security, Data Structures and more. I’ve always had the passion for helping others; my first degree provided me with the worldview to direct this passion, and my current degree is providing me with the tangible skills need to implement this passion.

Though many feel like my path is a bit all over the place, I feel like I’m building a bigger picture of what software engineering can and should be; a place where people, programming, and policy combine.
