Project Description

    Something I’ve dedicated many hours to over the last 5 years is mental health advocacy and stigma eradication. Much of that work was inspired by and in collaboration with Jack.org - an organization for youth mental health. I was an early student volunteer with them, spearheading my University of Guelph chapter of the organization, participating in their pilot year (and two more after that) of speakers programs, and even interning as a fundraising coordinator for two summers in a row. During this work, I met my friend, Ameera. She is very active in this space as well, and for years her and I have chatted about starting a podcast about our experiences with mental health and mental illness.

    This past summer we finally went for it! Every Saturday for two months, we congregated at the Digital Innovation Hub at the Toronto Public Library and recorded episodes about what it feels like to be high functioning but still struggle severely with mental illness.

    The last few months have been filled with edits, social media planning, and the occasional bout of cold feet. But it’s finally live and I can’t wait to see where this goes! At the very least, it’s been great practice to actually put something out in the world that you’ve been thinking about for some time - even if it’s vulnerable and could completely fail.
